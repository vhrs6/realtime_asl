American Sign Language

American Sign Language (ASL) is a complete, natural language used by the Deaf and hard-of-hearing community in the United States and parts of Canada. It uses hand gestures, facial expressions, and body movements to convey meaning, making it a visual-gestural language rather than spoken. ASL has its own grammar, syntax, and vocabulary, separate from English. It enables effective communication and cultural expression among Deaf individuals and their communities. Learning ASL promotes inclusivity, accessibility, and awareness of Deaf culture. ASL is widely taught in schools, interpreted in media, and used in various professional settings to support equal communication rights.
